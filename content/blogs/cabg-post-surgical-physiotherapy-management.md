## Understanding CABG Surgery and the Recovery Process

### What is CABG Surgery?

Coronary Artery Bypass Graft (CABG) surgery is a major cardiac procedure that creates new pathways around blocked or narrowed coronary arteries to restore blood flow to the heart muscle. This life-saving surgery involves harvesting healthy blood vessels from other parts of the patient’s body (typically the internal mammary artery, saphenous vein from the leg, or radial artery from the arm) and using them to create “bypasses” around the blocked coronary arteries.

**Types of CABG Procedures:**

- Traditional CABG: Performed with heart-lung machine (on-pump)
- Off-pump CABG: Performed on beating heart without heart-lung machine
- Minimally invasive CABG: Smaller incisions with specialized techniques
- Robotic-assisted CABG: Advanced robotic technology for precision

Common Indications for CABG:

- Multi-vessel coronary artery disease
- Left main coronary artery stenosis
- Failed angioplasty or stent procedures
- Acute coronary syndrome with complex anatomy
- Diabetic patients with multi-vessel disease

### The Surgical Impact on the Body

CABG surgery involves significant physiological stress and requires comprehensive recovery management:

Cardiovascular System Changes:

- Temporary reduction in cardiac function
- Altered heart rhythm patterns
- Blood pressure fluctuations
- Increased risk of blood clots
- Changes in circulation patterns

Respiratory System Impact:

- Reduced lung capacity from chest incision
- Potential pleural effusion or pneumonia risk
- Weakened respiratory muscles
- Pain affecting deep breathing patterns
- Anesthesia effects on lung function

Musculoskeletal Consequences:

- Sternum (breastbone) division requiring 6-8 weeks healing
- Chest muscle weakness and stiffness
- Shoulder and neck tension from surgical positioning
- Leg incision sites (if saphenous vein harvested)
- General deconditioning from bed rest

Psychological and Cognitive Effects:

- Post-surgical depression and anxiety
- Fear of physical activity and exertion
- Cognitive changes from anesthesia and surgery
- Sleep disturbances and fatigue
- Adjustment to lifestyle modifications

## Phases of Post-CABG Recovery and Physiotherapy Goals

### Phase I: Immediate Post-Operative Period (ICU to Discharge – Days 1-7)

Primary Goals:

- Prevent post-operative complications
- Initiate early mobilization safely
- Optimize respiratory function
- Begin cardiovascular conditioning

Physiotherapy Interventions:

- Respiratory therapy: Deep breathing exercises, incentive spirometry, coughing techniques
- Early mobilization: Bedside sitting, standing, short-distance walking
- Circulation promotion: Ankle pumps, leg exercises, compression stockings
- Pain management: Positioning, gentle movement, breathing techniques

Key Milestones:

- Extubation within 24-48 hours
- Sitting at bedside by day 1-2
- Walking 50-100 meters by day 2-3
- Stair climbing before discharge
- Independent basic activities of daily living

Safety Considerations:

- Continuous cardiac monitoring
- Blood pressure and heart rate parameters
- Sternum protection during movement
- Infection prevention protocols
- Medication management coordination

Primary Goals:

- Promote sternum healing and wound care

- Prevent complications and readmission
- Establish safe exercise routine

Physiotherapy Focus:

- Sternum protection education: Proper lifting, coughing, and movement techniques
- Graduated walking program: Progressive distance and duration increases
- Respiratory conditioning: Continued breathing exercises and airway clearance
- Range of motion: Gentle stretching for shoulders, chest, and incision sites
- Functional training: Safe performance of daily activities

Week 1-2 Objectives:

- Walking 5-10 minutes, 2-3 times daily
- Maintaining respiratory exercises
- Safe showering and personal care
- Proper incision care techniques
- Recognition of warning signs

Week 3-4 Objectives:

- Beginning of structured exercise routine

Week 5-6 Objectives:

- Walking 20-30 minutes continuously
- Return to light work activities (if applicable)
- Increased independence in daily activities
- Preparation for formal cardiac rehabilitation
- Confidence building in physical capabilities

## Role of Physiotherapy in CABG Recovery

### Cardiovascular Rehabilitation

Exercise Tolerance Improvement: Physiotherapy plays a crucial role in gradually and safely improving cardiovascular fitness after CABG surgery.

*Progressive Exercise Training:*

- Heart rate-guided exercise prescription
- Gradual intensity and duration increases
- Continuous monitoring for adverse responses
- Individualized progression based on recovery status
- Integration of patient preferences and lifestyle

Cardiac Function Optimization: Structured exercise programs help restore and potentially improve cardiac function beyond pre-surgical levels.

*Mechanisms of Improvement:*

- Enhanced coronary collateral circulation
- Improved cardiac output and stroke volume
- Reduced resting heart rate and blood pressure
- Better oxygen utilization by muscles
- Increased overall cardiovascular efficiency

### Respiratory System Recovery

Pulmonary Function Restoration: CABG surgery significantly impacts respiratory function, requiring targeted physiotherapy interventions.

*Respiratory Physiotherapy Components:*

- Deep breathing exercises: Prevent atelectasis and pneumonia
- Incentive spirometry: Encourage deep lung expansion
- Coughing techniques: Effective secretion clearance
- Postural drainage: If secretion retention occurs
- Breathing pattern optimization: Efficient respiratory mechanics

Prevention of Pulmonary Complications:

- Pneumonia prevention through airway clearance
- Atelectasis resolution through lung expansion
- Pleural effusion management
- Respiratory muscle strengthening
- Improved gas exchange efficiency

### Musculoskeletal System Rehabilitation

Sternum Healing Support: The divided sternum requires specific protection and graduated loading for optimal healing.

*Sternum Protection Strategies:*

- Education about lifting restrictions (typically <5kg for 6-8 weeks)
- Proper body mechanics for daily activities
- Safe coughing and sneezing techniques
- Gradual return to upper body activities
- Monitoring for signs of sternal instability

Overall Strength and Conditioning: Comprehensive muscle conditioning addresses surgery-related deconditioning.

*Strength Training Progression:*

- Weeks 1-6: Light activities, no lifting restrictions
- Weeks 6-8: Gradual introduction of light weights (1-2kg)
- Weeks 8-12: Progressive resistance training
- 3+ months: Full strength training program
- Ongoing: Maintenance strength training

### Psychological and Cognitive Support

Addressing Post-Surgical Depression: Cardiac surgery can trigger depression and anxiety requiring specialized intervention.

*Psychological Support Strategies:*

- Education about normal emotional responses
- Goal setting and achievement recognition
- Social support facilitation
- Stress management techniques
- Professional counseling referral when needed

Cognitive Function Recovery: Some patients experience temporary cognitive changes after cardiac surgery.

*Cognitive Rehabilitation Approaches:*

- Memory and attention training exercises
- Gradual return to complex tasks
- Family education about cognitive changes
- Patience and understanding during recovery
- Professional neuropsychological assessment if needed

## Specific Physiotherapy Interventions for CABG Patients

### Aerobic Exercise Training

Walking Programs: Walking forms the foundation of cardiac rehabilitation for CABG patients.

*Progressive Walking Protocol:*

- Week 1: 5-10 minutes, 2-3 times daily
- Week 2: 10-15 minutes, 2 times daily
- Week 3: 15-20 minutes, 2 times daily
- Week 4: 20-25 minutes, 1-2 times daily
- Week 5-6: 25-30 minutes daily
- Week 7+: 30-45 minutes, 5-6 days weekly

Heart Rate Monitoring: Safe exercise intensity determination through heart rate guidelines.

*Target Heart Rate Calculation:*

- Formula: (220 – age – 10) × 0.6-0.8 for moderate intensity
- RPE Scale: Rating of Perceived Exertion 11-14 (moderate intensity)
- Talk Test: Ability to hold conversation during exercise
- Symptoms: No chest pain, severe shortness of breath, or dizziness

Stationary Cycling: Low-impact cardiovascular exercise suitable for CABG patients.

*Cycling Progression:*

- Start with 5-10 minutes at low resistance
- Gradually increase duration before increasing intensity
- Monitor heart rate and symptoms throughout
- Consider recumbent bikes for back support
- Progress to 20-45 minutes of continuous cycling

### Resistance Training

Upper Body Strengthening: Gradual reintroduction of upper body exercises after sternum healing.

*Upper Body Exercise Progression:*

- Weeks 1-6: No lifting >2-3kg, focus on range of motion
- Weeks 6-8: Light resistance bands and 1-2kg weights
- Weeks 8-12: Progressive weight increases to 5-10kg
- 3+ months: Full resistance training program
- Ongoing: Maintenance strength training

Lower Body Strengthening: Lower body exercises can begin earlier as they don’t stress the sternum.

*Lower Body Exercise Examples:*

- Chair stands and sit-to-stand exercises
- Heel and toe raises for calf strengthening
- Light leg weights for quadriceps and hamstrings
- Wall slides for gluteal strengthening
- Step-ups on low steps or platforms

Core Stabilization: Careful core strengthening that protects the healing sternum.

*Safe Core Exercises:*

- Deep breathing with abdominal engagement
- Gentle pelvic tilts and bridges
- Modified planks against walls
- Seated core exercises with proper support
- Progressive return to full core training after 3 months

### Flexibility and Mobility Training

Chest and Shoulder Mobility: Addressing tightness and scarring from surgical incisions.

*Mobility Exercises:*

- Gentle arm circles and shoulder rolls
- Cross-body arm stretches
- Doorway chest stretches (after 6 weeks)
- Overhead reaching exercises
- Neck and shoulder relaxation techniques

Spinal Mobility: Maintaining spine flexibility and addressing posture changes.

*Spinal Mobility Techniques:*

- Gentle spinal rotation exercises
- Cat-cow stretches on hands and knees
- Seated spinal extension exercises
- Thoracic spine mobility exercises
- Postural awareness training

### Functional Training

Activities of Daily Living: Helping patients safely return to normal daily activities.

*ADL Training Components:*

- Safe lifting and carrying techniques
- Stair climbing with proper pacing
- Household activity modifications
- Driving readiness assessment
- Work activity simulation

Recreational Activity Preparation: Gradual return to hobbies and recreational pursuits.

*Recreation Considerations:*

- Golf swing modifications and timing
- Swimming pool entry and exit safety
- Gardening adaptations and tool use
- Travel considerations and precautions
- Social activity participation guidelines

## Precautions and Safety Guidelines for CABG Recovery

### Exercise Safety Parameters

Cardiac Monitoring Guidelines: Safe exercise requires continuous attention to cardiac responses.

*Heart Rate Guidelines:*

- Target heart rate: 60-80% of maximum safe heart rate
- Resting heart rate should return to normal within 10 minutes
- Avoid exercise if resting heart rate >100 bpm
- Stop exercise if heart rate exceeds prescribed maximum
- Monitor for irregular heart rhythms

*Blood Pressure Considerations:*

- Pre-exercise blood pressure should be <180/100 mmHg
- Post-exercise blood pressure should return to baseline within 10 minutes
- Avoid exercise if systolic BP >200 mmHg during activity
- Monitor for orthostatic hypotension
- Coordinate with medication timing

Symptom Recognition and Response: *Warning Signs Requiring Immediate Medical Attention:*

- Chest pain or pressure: Especially if similar to pre-surgical symptoms
- Severe shortness of breath: Beyond normal exercise response
- Dizziness or lightheadedness: Particularly with exercise
- Irregular heart rhythms: Palpitations or skipped beats
- Excessive fatigue: Unusual tiredness lasting >24 hours

*Exercise Modification Indicators:*

- Mild chest discomfort during activity
- Shortness of breath that resolves with rest
- Muscle fatigue or joint discomfort
- Weather-related breathing difficulties
- Medication side effects affecting exercise

### Sternum Protection and Wound Care

Lifting and Movement Restrictions: Proper sternum protection is crucial for healing and preventing complications.

*Lifting Guidelines:*

- Weeks 1-6: Maximum 2-3 kg (5-7 lbs)
- Weeks 6-8: Gradual increase to 5 kg (11 lbs)
- Weeks 8-12: Progressive increase based on healing
- 3+ months: Return to normal lifting with proper technique
- Permanent: Avoid sudden, jerky movements

*Movement Techniques:*

- Use leg muscles for lifting, not arms
- Avoid pushing or pulling heavy objects
- Roll out of bed rather than sitting up abruptly
- Use both arms equally for activities
- Avoid reaching overhead until cleared

Incision Care and Monitoring: *Daily Wound Assessment:*

- Check for signs of infection (redness, swelling, drainage)
- Monitor healing progress and report concerns
- Keep incisions clean and dry
- Avoid submersion in water until cleared
- Follow specific dressing change instructions

*Infection Prevention:*

- Proper hand hygiene before touching incisions
- Avoid exposure to sick individuals
- Maintain good nutrition for healing
- Follow antibiotic instructions if prescribed
- Report fever >101°F (38.3°C) immediately

### Medication Management and Exercise

Cardiac Medication Considerations: Many cardiac medications affect exercise response and require adjustments.

*Common Medication Effects:*

- Beta-blockers: Reduce heart rate response to exercise
- ACE inhibitors: May cause dizziness with position changes
- Diuretics: Can cause dehydration and electrolyte imbalances
- Anticoagulants: Increase bleeding risk with injury
- Pain medications: May mask symptoms or cause drowsiness

*Exercise Timing with Medications:*

- Plan exercise around medication schedules
- Monitor for side effects affecting exercise safety
- Stay well-hydrated, especially with diuretics
- Report any concerning symptoms to healthcare team
- Never stop medications without physician approval

## Frequently Asked Questions

### When can I start physiotherapy after CABG surgery?

Physiotherapy typically begins within 24-48 hours after CABG surgery while still in the hospital. Initial interventions focus on breathing exercises, gentle mobilization, and circulation improvement. Home-based physiotherapy usually starts 1-2 weeks after discharge once basic wound healing is established and the patient is medically stable. Early initiation of physiotherapy significantly improves outcomes and reduces complications. The timing may be adjusted based on surgical complexity, complications, and individual recovery status. Most patients benefit from continuing physiotherapy for 3-6 months post-surgery.
