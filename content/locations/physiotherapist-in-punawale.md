### Is home physiotherapy as effective as clinic-based therapy?

Yes. Home physiotherapy delivers the same evidence-based treatments (exercises, manual therapy, stretches, modalities) you'd get in a clinic. In fact, research shows that for many conditions (such as post-operative rehab), home-based rehabilitation achieves outcomes as good as outpatient clinic therapy. The difference is convenience and personal attention—getting full focus from the therapist in your own space can often enhance your results.

### What conditions can be treated with home physiotherapy?

A wide range of issues can be treated at home. This includes orthopedic problems (back pain, neck pain, knee/shoulder injuries, arthritis, sprains), sports injuries (ligament tears like ACL, muscle strains, tendonitis), post-surgery recovery (hip/knee replacement, spine surgery rehab), neurological disorders (stroke rehab, Parkinson's, MS, cerebral palsy, neuropathy), pediatric concerns (developmental delays, childhood injuries) and chronic pain conditions (fibromyalgia, long-term back pain). Essentially, any movement or function problem affecting daily life can often improve with physiotherapy. During your first session, the therapist will assess your needs and confirm whether home therapy is suitable.

### How many physiotherapy sessions will I need to recover?

It depends on your condition's severity, your age/health, and your recovery goals. Mild injuries or simple pain may improve in a few sessions, while serious surgeries or chronic conditions may require months of therapy. Most patients start feeling better (less pain, better mobility) within 2–3 sessions. We usually recommend an initial plan of around 10 sessions, then reassess progress. Your therapist will estimate a plan during the assessment. Remember, doing prescribed exercises at home between sessions significantly boosts your recovery speed.

### How should I prepare for my at-home physiotherapy visit?

Preparing is easy. Choose a quiet, comfortable spot at home (like a living room or bedroom) with enough clear floor space for exercises. Wear loose, stretchable clothing (T-shirt and shorts or track pants) so you can move freely. Keep a water bottle and towel nearby. If you have any relevant medical reports or prior imaging (X-rays, MRI), have them ready to show the therapist. Also let us know if you use any assistive devices (cane, walker, knee brace). You don't need to buy equipment—our therapists bring basic tools (bands, therapy balls) as needed. With just a little space and comfort, we'll take care of the rest.
