### How should I prepare for an at-home physiotherapy visit?

Preparing for a home physio session is easy and doesn't require any special equipment in most cases. Here are a few tips to get ready:

- **Choose a comfortable space:** Clear a little area in your home for the session – typically a living room or bedroom with enough floor space. Move aside any fragile items. If you have an exercise mat or a firm bed, that can be useful for exercises done lying down.
- **Wear comfortable clothing:** Put on loose-fitting, stretchable clothes that allow free movement (e.g. T-shirt/shorts or track pants). This lets you exercise without restriction and allows the therapist to evaluate your movement properly.
- **Have basic items handy:** Keep a bottle of water and a towel nearby. If you have any prior medical reports or doctor's prescription related to your condition, have those ready to show the physiotherapist. Also, if you use any assistive devices (like a cane, walker, or knee brace), mention it to the therapist.
- **Environment:** Ensure the room is at a comfortable temperature. You might switch off distractions like TV during the session so you can focus. If you have pets, it might help to keep them in another room during the treatment to avoid interruptions (we love pets, but it's safer to have a calm environment for exercises!).
- **Communication:** Think about the symptoms or questions you want to discuss. During the first visit especially, be ready to describe your pain or mobility issue in detail. The more we know, the better we can help.
