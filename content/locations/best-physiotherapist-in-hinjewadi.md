### How should I prepare for an at-home physiotherapy visit?

Preparing for a home physio session is easy and doesn't require any special equipment in most cases. Here are a few tips to get ready:

- **Choose a comfortable space:** Clear a small area in your home for the session – typically a living room or bedroom with enough floor space for a yoga mat or for you to lie down comfortably. Move aside any fragile items or obstacles. If you have an exercise mat or a firm bed, that can be useful for exercises that need to be done lying down.
- **Wear comfortable clothing:** Put on loose-fitting, stretchable clothes that allow free movement (e.g. a t-shirt with shorts or track pants). This lets you exercise without restriction and also allows the therapist to observe your joint movements and posture.
- **Keep basic items handy:** Have a bottle of water and a towel nearby. If you have any prior medical reports, X-rays/MRI, or doctor's prescriptions related to your condition, keep those ready to show the physiotherapist. Also, if you use any assistive devices (like a knee brace, cane, or walker), mention it to the therapist and have them available during the session.
- **Set the environment:** Ensure the room is at a comfortable temperature and free from distractions. You might switch off the TV or silence your phone during the session so you can focus. If you have pets, consider keeping them in another room for the duration of the appointment – we love pets, but it's safer and less distracting to have a calm space for exercises!
- **Communicate your needs:** Think about the symptoms, questions, or goals you want to discuss with the physiotherapist. During the first visit especially, be ready to describe your pain or mobility issue in detail and what activities it affects (for example, "I can't sit for more than 30 minutes without back pain"). The more information you provide, the better we can tailor the treatment plan to you. Remember, this is one-on-one time for **you**, so don't hesitate to voice any concerns or ask questions.
